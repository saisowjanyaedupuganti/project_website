Visualizations

1. Accident Map – Number of Casualties

Each point shows a crash location in the UK. The colour and density of points highlight accident hotspots.

2. Average Speed Limit by Accident Severity

This bar chart compares average speed limits for accidents with different severity levels.

3. Number of Accidents by Day of Week

Here we count accidents for each day of the week to see which days are riskier.

4. Distribution of Accidents by Light Conditions

The bubble chart shows how many accidents happen in daylight, darkness and other light conditions.

5. Accidents by Junction Control Type

This treemap splits accidents by junction control, such as signals, give-way or uncontrolled junctions.

6. Geographic Distribution of Vehicles Involved

A second map focusing on how many vehicles are involved at each accident location.

7. Yearly Trend of Accidents by Weather Conditions

Stacked bars show how accident counts change over years and how they are split by weather conditions.

8. Distribution of Accidents by Road Surface Conditions

This pie chart shows the share of casualties on dry, wet, flooded and other road surfaces.

9. Scatter Plot – Number of Vehicles vs Number of Casualties

Each point represents one accident. It helps us see how the number of vehicles relates to the number of casualties.

10. Police Force by Weather Conditions

This chart estimates how much police effort is associated with accidents in different weather conditions.
